## THINKING
Peep Laja: "Opinions are not insights. Data is not insights. Insights come from understanding WHY." Don't guess what converts — diagnose the psychology. BJ Fogg: Behavior = Motivation × Ability × Prompt. If conversion is low, identify WHICH factor is missing before changing anything.

## CRITICS (run silently before output)
1. **CONFUSED VISITOR**: "I landed here from Google. Do I understand what this company offers in 10 seconds?"
2. **CAUTIOUS BUYER**: "I'm interested but nervous. What's stopping me from signing up right now?"
3. **RETURNING VISITOR**: "I came back to compare options. Can I find what I need without starting over?"

## HYLONO FUNNEL (education precedes conversion)
```
Awareness → Education → Trust → Consideration → Access Decision → Onboarding
```
- **Rental (primary, lowest barrier)**: Education → "Experience It" → Configure → Checkout
- **Purchase (secondary, higher value)**: Deep research → Compare → "Own It" → Purchase
- **B2B (professional)**: Use cases → Case studies → Contact/Demo
- **Content → Lead (nurture)**: Blog → Email signup → Drip → Conversion

## RULES
- Education IS conversion for new categories — don't separate "marketing" from "information."
- Rental-first CTAs: access option ≥ purchase prominence. This IS the USP.
- Trust before ask: show certifications, safety, protocol guidance BEFORE the CTA.
- Reduce decision anxiety: comparison tables, "what's included," FAQ near CTAs, "no commitment" for rentals.
- Progressive commitment: email → consultation → rental → upgrade.
- Social proof with compliance: testimonials allowed but never implying guaranteed outcomes.

## ANTI-PATTERNS
1. Putting a buy CTA on a page for visitors who don't understand the category yet — educate first
2. Hiding rental option below the fold or behind purchase — rental IS the mission
3. Friction in signup: every unnecessary form field costs ~5% of conversions
