## Executive Summary

This codebase has a decent SEO foundation at the implementation level: canonical helpers exist, route metadata is centralized, structured-data governance checks pass, and the production build succeeds. Those are meaningful positives.

The current SEO ceiling is set by deployment and indexation issues, not by missing polish. The two biggest blockers are:

1. the configured primary domain is `hylono.eu`, but that hostname did not resolve during the audit, while `hylono.com` serves a different WordPress "coming soon" site, and
2. dynamic content routes are implemented as indexable soft-404s instead of true 404 responses.

Until those are fixed, the site cannot earn or keep clean indexation signals.

Hard cap applied:
- Public-domain/deployment split
- Indexable soft-404 behavior on core dynamic templates

## Highest-Priority Findings

### 1. Public SEO is split across an unresolved `.eu` hostname and a different `.com` site

Severity: Critical

Evidence:
- The app defaults its primary site URL to `https://hylono.eu` in [`lib/env.ts`](../lib/env.ts).
- During this audit, `https://hylono.eu` did not resolve from the environment.
- `https://hylono.com` returned `200`, served a WordPress "Pardon our dust!" holding page, exposed `wp-sitemap.xml`, and used WordPress robots rules.
- Current route metadata and JSON-LD in this repo point to `https://hylono.eu`, not `https://hylono.com`.

Why it matters:
- Search engines need one stable, crawlable primary host.
- Right now the codebase, the configured canonical host, and the live reachable host do not align.
- That can invalidate canonicals, split entity signals, and prevent the intended app from being indexed at all.

Business impact:
- This is a hard blocker for organic visibility until deployment/domain ownership is unified.

### 2. Dynamic routes generate indexable soft-404 pages instead of returning true 404 responses

Severity: Critical

Evidence:
- Product route metadata is generated for any slug in [`app/product/[tech]/page.tsx`](../app/product/%5Btech%5D/page.tsx), while the client renders `<ErrorPage type="404" />` for invalid enum values in [`app/product/[tech]/ProductClient.tsx`](../app/product/%5Btech%5D/ProductClient.tsx).
- Condition, protocol, and blog routes follow the same pattern: metadata is always generated in [`app/conditions/[slug]/page.tsx`](../app/conditions/%5Bslug%5D/page.tsx), [`app/protocols/[slug]/page.tsx`](../app/protocols/%5Bslug%5D/page.tsx), and [`app/blog/[slug]/page.tsx`](../app/blog/%5Bslug%5D/page.tsx), while client fallbacks handle missing content later.
- The repo's captured rendered product artifact `.tmp-product.html` shows `/product/hbot-st1700` with:
  - `200` status in the audit summary
  - `index, follow`
  - canonical `https://hylono.eu/product/hbot-st1700`
  - JSON-LD for a normal page
  - visible "Page Not Found" UI in the body

Why it matters:
- This creates classic soft-404 behavior: invalid URLs get indexable metadata, canonical tags, internal-link context, and noindex is not applied.
- Search engines waste crawl budget and may index junk URLs, especially where slug guesses or casing variations are possible.

Business impact:
- This issue can affect every dynamic content area, not just one product.

### 3. Blog structured data uses the wrong hostname and wrong page identifiers

Severity: High

Evidence:
- Blog pages use legacy client-side schema components in [`components/BlogArticle.tsx`](../components/BlogArticle.tsx).
- Those components build breadcrumb URLs with `https://hylono.com/...` and article `mainEntityOfPage.@id` values under `https://hylono.com/blog/...` in [`components/StructuredData.tsx`](../components/StructuredData.tsx).
- The same repo otherwise generates canonical URLs against `https://hylono.eu`.

Why it matters:
- Article structured data should reinforce canonical page identity, not point to a different host.
- Right now article metadata, breadcrumb entity paths, and canonical host strategy are inconsistent.
- Because `.com` is live and `.eu` is not publicly reachable here, this conflict is not theoretical.

Business impact:
- Blog content is one of the easiest organic acquisition surfaces; conflicting host/entity signals reduce discoverability and snippet confidence.

### 4. The locator page is client-only and under-specified for local SEO

Severity: Medium

Evidence:
- The main locator component is imported with `ssr: false` in [`app/locator/LocatorClient.tsx`](../app/locator/LocatorClient.tsx).
- The route itself only sets metadata in [`app/locator/page.tsx`](../app/locator/page.tsx); it does not emit `LocalBusiness`, `Place`, `MedicalBusiness`, or branch/location schema.
- The captured HTML for `.tmp-locator.html` shows canonical and generic Organization/WebSite JSON-LD, but no location-specific structured data.

Why it matters:
- Local/distributor/clinic discovery pages benefit from server-rendered location summaries and location-specific entity markup.
- Client-only rendering is a weaker foundation for local intent pages than crawlable server-rendered content.

Business impact:
- This limits the locator's ability to support local-intent queries, partner discovery, and location rich results.

### 5. Title strategy is still too generic on several indexable templates

Severity: Medium

Evidence:
- Multiple route titles are single-word labels:
  - [`app/store/page.tsx`](../app/store/page.tsx): `Store`
  - [`app/research/page.tsx`](../app/research/page.tsx): `Research`
  - [`app/faq/page.tsx`](../app/faq/page.tsx): `FAQ`
  - [`app/about/page.tsx`](../app/about/page.tsx): `About`
- Rendered samples in `.tmp-seo-browser-summary.json` confirm titles like `Store`, `Research`, and `Partner Locator`.

Why it matters:
- These titles are functional but not competitive in SERPs.
- They underuse available space for primary intent, geography, category, or value proposition.

Business impact:
- CTR and query relevance are likely lower than they need to be, even where pages are otherwise indexable.
